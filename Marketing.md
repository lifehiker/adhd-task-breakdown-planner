# Marketing

## FocusSteps - ADHD Task Breakdown Planner

---

## Positioning & Taglines

- **Primary:** "Start the thing. Finish it. No planning required."
- **Alt 1:** "Turn any task into 5 tiny steps. Start in under a minute."
- **Alt 2:** "The ADHD productivity tool that doesn't make you plan before you work."
- **Alt 3:** "One task. Small steps. Done."

**Core differentiator:** Unlike Notion, Todoist, or Tiimo — FocusSteps requires zero setup. You type one task, it breaks it down instantly with AI, and you start working. No lists, no projects, no friction.

---

## Target Audience

- Adults with ADHD (diagnosed or self-identified), ages 22-40
- Freelancers, students, remote workers, creators
- People who own Notion but never use it
- People who have tried and abandoned 5+ productivity apps
- People who know what they need to do but can't start

## Competitor Gaps

| Competitor | What They Lack That We Have |
|---|---|
| Goblin Tools | No timer, no session flow, no subscription model, no AI continuation |
| Tiimo | Visual scheduler, not execution-focused; no AI breakdown |
| Structured | Requires manual planning; no AI; expensive |
| Focusmate | Body doubling only, no task breakdown |
| Notion AI | Too complex, no ADHD-specific UX, high friction |
| Standard Pomodoro apps | No task breakdown, user still has to figure out what to do |

**Our edge:** Only app that combines (1) single-task focus, (2) instant AI breakdown, (3) step-by-step guided execution, (4) "make it easier" escape hatch, and (5) ADHD-specific design (no lists, no planning overhead).

## Viral Hooks

- **The 30-second demo:** Show typing a task -> AI breaks it down -> starting the timer. Extremely shareable on TikTok/Reels.
- **"Make it easier" button:** Very relatable to ADHD users — screenshot/video of clicking "make it easier" to make an already-small step smaller resonates deeply.
- **Completion celebration screen:** Trophy + step count is feel-good shareable content.
- **Relatable copy:** "You already know what you need to do. We just help you start it." — tweet/post format.

## High-Priority Flags

**HIGH PRIORITY: r/ADHD Reddit** - 1.8M members, highly engaged, responds well to authentic "I built this for myself" posts. Low competition for execution tools (most tools posted are complex planners, not execution aids). Direct feature-market fit.

**HIGH PRIORITY: TikTok ADHD creators** - ADHD content on TikTok has 10B+ views. A short demo video showing task paralysis -> one click -> working in 60 seconds is highly shareable. Low production cost, potentially viral.
